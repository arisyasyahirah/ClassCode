Beyond Just "Choosing a Font"

Typography accounts for over 90% of web design. It is the primary way we communicate information to users. Mastering text properties ensures your site is readable, accessible, and professional.

1. Understanding Font Families

Browsers use a "Fallback" system. If the first font isn't available, it moves to the next one.

Category Characteristics Best For...
Serif Small "feet" (serifs) on characters. Traditional, elegant, print-like feel.
Sans-Serif Clean, straight edges. No feet. Modern look, high digital readability.
Monospace Every character has the same width. Code snippets and technical data.

2. Sizing Units (Exam Focus)

Choosing the right unit is critical for Responsive Design:

  • PX (Pixels): Absolute unit. Fixed size that doesn't change.
  • EM: Relative to the font-size of its direct parent.
  • REM (Root EM): Relative to the <html> tag font-size. Highly recommended for accessibility.

Study Tip: Line-Height

Always set a line-height between 1.5 and 1.8 for body text. This prevents "line crowding" and makes long paragraphs much easier for the human brain to process.

Mastering CSS

Typography is not just about choosing a font. It's about spacing, sizing, and creating a clear path for the reader's eyes to follow. When you change the letter-spacing, notice how the "breathability" of the text changes.

Pro Student Study Strategy

How to pass your CSS Exam:

  • Contrast: If someone squinted at your site, could they tell the difference between a title and a paragraph? If not, increase the font-weight or font-size.
  • The 60-75 Rule: The most readable lines of text are 60 to 75 characters wide. Use max-width on your text containers!
  • Web Safe Fonts: Remember that Arial, Times New Roman, and Verdana are "Web Safe" because almost every computer has them installed.
